Abstract

This paper presents BOW-CONNECT, a novel approach for efficient robotic exploration and mapping using bag-of-words based connectivity detection. Our method leverages visual bag-of-words representations to identify potential loop closures and connectivity between different regions of the environment.

The proposed system integrates bag-of-words features with robot exploration strategies to improve mapping efficiency and accuracy. By detecting connectivity patterns in real-time, our approach enables robots to make informed decisions about exploration paths and optimize the mapping process.

We demonstrate the effectiveness of BOW-CONNECT through extensive experiments in various environments. Our results show significant improvements in exploration efficiency, map quality, and computational performance compared to baseline methods. The system is capable of handling large-scale environments while maintaining real-time performance.
